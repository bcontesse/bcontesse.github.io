Projects

Pathfinding Visualizer

This particular project was born out of necessity while studying how different search algorithms conduct their searches. It began as a though experiment to see if I could implement the most common search algorithms in Python. However, it became difficult to understand if my implementations, especially for A*, were working correctly by just printing out results.

The answer was to develop an HTML/Javascript visualization of the different searches in order to test and visually confirm the algorithms. By sharing this app on my website, I hope to increase other people's understanding of these algorithms, especially anyone studying for technical interviews.

2020

Sigep Coco Booth

Booth is a Carnegie Mellon tradition that consists of constructing a two-story wooden building over the course of one week. This building is then decorated according to a specific theme, in this case the Disney movie Coco, for visitors to walk through and experience. For my fraternity, I was the head Booth Chair in charge of organizing the design and construction of this massive undertaking, a process which took over 6 months. I handled budgeting for materials, fundraising, organizing build sessions, as well as guiding the design of each room and the overall structure.

Our booth consisted of multiple themed roomes including an ofrenda, a plaza, a pathway to the 'afterlife', and two concerts. The building had running electricity, music, a guitar-controlled simon says game and mutliple interactive buttons to weave the Coco narrative. Of all fraternity booths, ours won first place and was arguably the most visited and talked-about booth of the event.

2019

Galage 2.0

For my introductory programming course we were given freedom to create whatever we wanted using Python as a final project. Since I have always been transfixed by videogames, I decided to try creating one myself using the building blocks of one of my favorites. Though Galaga was big in the 80s, it has special importance for me since it was a game that my brother showed me and we would play for hours whenever we would find it.

Using Pygame and a plethora of assets from other games, I created my own Galaga experience. Some highlights that differentiate it from the original is the ability to: upgrade your ship, have simultaneous players, and control the aliens and their paths against an AI ship. This was my way of creating an experience my brother and I could truly play together instead of just taking turns.

About Me

Hi! My name is Ben Contesse and I like programming. Specifically, I enjoy coding solutions to problems that makes people’s lives easier. In my current role as an Optimization Engineer at ExxonMobil, I’ve had a great opportunity to do exactly that. My day-to-day work involves developing tools that make use of Python and SQL to quickly generate and analyze optimization models.

Recently, I graduated from Carnegie Mellon University with a B.S. in Chemical Engineering and a Concentration in Computer Science. It was in my freshman year that I learned how to code, and I haven’t stopped since then.

When I’m not coding something to solve a problem, I enjoy creating things to entertain others. I’ve always spent a ton of time playing video games, so trying to develop my own was the logical next step. Entertaining others has also extended to creating playlists on Spotify, hosting others for game nights, and even designing and building my version of Disney’s Coco.

I have a drive to create and am looking for that next project to take on. I hope to grow into more opportunities that allow my skillset to flourish in order to tackle more complex and interesting issues. Feel free to reach out to me here!
