About Me

Hi! My name is Ben Contesse and I like programming. Specifically, I enjoy coding solutions to problems that makes people’s lives easier. In my current role as an Optimization Engineer at ExxonMobil, I’ve had a great opportunity to do exactly that. My day-to-day work involves developing tools that make use of Python and SQL to quickly generate and analyze optimization models.

Recently, I graduated from Carnegie Mellon University with a B.S. in Chemical Engineering and a Concentration in Computer Science. It was in my freshman year that I learned how to code, and I haven’t stopped since then.

When I’m not coding something to solve a problem, I enjoy creating things to entertain others. I’ve always spent a ton of time playing video games, so trying to develop my own was the logical next step. Entertaining others has also extended to creating playlists on Spotify, hosting others for game nights, and even designing and building my version of Disney’s Coco.

I have a drive to create and am looking for that next project to take on. I hope to grow into more opportunities that allow my skillset to flourish in order to tackle more complex and interesting issues.
